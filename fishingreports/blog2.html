Incredible Tailing Redfish Action!

POSTED ON August 18, 2016 BY FLATADDICTED


Huge Mosquito Lagoon Red Fish are here in Florida once again and that means it’s time for the fall Mullet run. The Mullet run triggers a feeding frenzy for big bull redfish in the north end of the Indian River and parts of the Mosquito Lagoon. This is the last time the red fish will have an abundance of free food on the flats before the mullet move away, they sense this which triggers the feed. It’s not uncommon to get into schools of five to six hundred red fish that weigh between 20 and 40 pounds.

The Mosquito Lagoon and Indian River are well-known for this phenomenon worldwide. Anglers have been targeting them for well over 30 years attempting to best their personal records with spinning or fly fishing gear. When it comes to fishing with conventional or spinning gear the red fish will take topwater plugs and live bait fish on the surface. Fly fishermen like to use top water flies like poppers or simulated plugs as well as bait fish style flies like seaducers and clousers.

From now until the end of September and even sometimes the middle of October the spawning redfish will be the main target. Over the years this has by far become my favorite time to be a fishing guide because it just simply cannot be outdone. There is absolutely nothing more exciting than watching a 30-pound redfish explode on the surface in a school of 600 fish all fighting for the same lure or bait fish… even after 12 years of guiding I still get excited seeing it.
